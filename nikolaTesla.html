Nikola Tesla

The Father of AC Electricity


The Life of Nikola Tesla:

  • 1856: Nikola Tesla was born on July 10 in the Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia).

  • 1861: Tesla attended primary school. Tesla was able to do Calculus in his head, which lead teachers to believe he was cheating.

  • 1873: Graduating, Tesla finishes a four year school term in 3 years. Shortly after, Tesla contracts cholera and is nearly killed by it.

  • 1882: Offered a job working with the Continental Edison Company in Paris.

  • 1888: George Westinghouse buys the patent rights to Tesla's polyphase system of AC dynamos, motors, and transformers.

  • 1891: Tesla becomes a US citizen, and invents the Tesla coil. The Tesla coil is still widely used in modern electronics such as the television and radio.

  • 1893: Westinghouse utilizes Tesla's AC system to power the World's Columbian Exposition in Chicago.

  • 1898: Tesla invents a remote controlled boat, and shows it in use to a crowd in Madison Square Garden.

  • 1899: Tesla discovers terrestrial stationary waves, proving the Earth could be used as a conductor and made to resonate an electrical frequency.

  • 1900: Tesla began construction of a wireless brodcasting tower, financed by JP Morgan. Unfortanately, the project was abandoned due to a financial scare, labour issues, and Morgan's widthdrawl of support.

  • 1943: Tesla passes away at the age of 86, in his hotel room in New York. Shortly after his death, the FBI confiscated his belongings.
